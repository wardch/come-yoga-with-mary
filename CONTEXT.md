# Come Yoga With Mary

This context defines the public language for Mary Duffy's personal brand and website. It keeps the site focused on a person-led creator and educator brand rather than a single local yoga class.

## Language

**Mary Duffy**:
The person-led brand at the intersection of organisational psychology, burnout recovery, and embodied wellbeing. Mary is a qualified organisational psychologist and certified yoga teacher.
_Avoid_: Candlelit Yoga as the primary brand, yoga class business, generic wellness influencer

**Creator and Educator Brand**:
A public platform where Mary teaches evidence-based ideas through social content, email, workshops, mini series, playlists, and future paid offers. The brand is built around trust, clarity, and audience growth before monetisation.
_Avoid_: therapy practice, clinical service, corporate consultancy as the primary frame

**Credential Boundary**:
Mary's credentials should be presented as qualified organisational psychologist and certified yoga teacher only if Mary is comfortable with that wording publicly. The website frames her work as educational and practical, not clinical therapy, diagnosis, treatment, or medical advice.
_Avoid_: therapy offer, medical advice, diagnosis, treatment claims

**Credibility Proof**:
Version 1 should prove trust through Mary's real credentials, her psychology-and-yoga bridge, credible external sources, and active social publishing. Testimonials, client logos, audience numbers, and outcome claims should appear only when they are true and permissioned.
_Avoid_: fake social proof, inflated claims, unpermissioned logos

**Burnout Recovery**:
The core audience problem Mary speaks to: people and workplaces trying to recover from depletion and build healthier ways of working. It connects organisational psychology with mind-body practices without presenting itself as medical treatment.
_Avoid_: cure, treatment, diagnosis

**Email List**:
The primary owned audience channel for people who want deeper guidance from Mary beyond social platforms. Social channels should point people toward the email list.
_Avoid_: newsletter as an afterthought, contact form as the main conversion

**Burnout Recovery Reset**:
The first lead magnet: a short, practical 5-day email series for high-performing people who feel constantly switched on. It introduces burnout signals, stress and recovery cycles, nervous system resets, work boundaries, and sustainable recovery rhythms.
_Avoid_: generic newsletter signup, vague free tips

**Kit**:
The email platform for forms, subscriber tags, automated lead magnet sequences, and ongoing weekly emails. The website should send new subscribers into Kit rather than implementing custom email delivery.
_Avoid_: custom email automation, newsletter platform undecided

**Signup Block**:
The Version 1 email capture for the Burnout Recovery Reset. It asks for email only, uses the button copy "Get the 5-day reset", and is designed to be swapped into a Kit form or embed once the Kit account is configured.
_Avoid_: custom database, custom email sender, long signup forms

**Email Cadence**:
The Burnout Recovery Reset is delivered as an immediate weekday sequence, followed by an ongoing weekly email. The default weekly send time is Tuesday at 8:30am Europe/Dublin until real audience data suggests a better time.
_Avoid_: sending the 5-day reset once per week, irregular newsletters

**Version 1 Website**:
A single conversion-focused homepage that turns social attention into email subscribers. It introduces Mary, explains the core promise, captures signups for the Burnout Recovery Reset, shows credibility, and points to social platforms and future offers.
_Avoid_: blog-first site, multi-page content hub, class schedule as the homepage focus

**Visual Direction**:
The site should feel calm, editorial, and professional: a grounded writer-educator brand for evidence-based wellbeing. Mary should be the first visual signal, with warm neutrals, deep ink, muted green, restrained accents, and candlelit yoga imagery used only as supporting context.
_Avoid_: spa branding, generic wellness templates, corporate stock photography, candlelit class imagery as the dominant signal

**Offer Ladder**:
Mary's audience journey starts with free social content, the Burnout Recovery Reset, and weekly email, then grows into low-cost workshops, mid-cost mini series, higher-cost workplace sessions, and premium offers later. The Version 1 website should hint at future offers without moving attention away from the free reset.
_Avoid_: starting with expensive corporate workshops, over-selling before trust exists

**Homepage Promise**:
For people who are good at coping, but not recovering. This frames Mary's work around the difference between pushing through and rebuilding sustainable recovery.
_Avoid_: recover from burnout without abandoning your ambition, vague mind-body wellness

**Content Pillars**:
Mary's public content is organised around burnout literacy, recovery as a skill, healthier work, and embodied leadership. Yoga belongs inside embodied leadership rather than standing alone as the main content category.
_Avoid_: yoga tips as the primary pillar, generic wellness content

**First Audience**:
Ambitious professionals, managers, founders, and team leads who are functioning but depleted. They want language for what is happening to them, practical recovery tools, and evidence-based ways to work without burning out again.
_Avoid_: everyone interested in wellness, yoga-only students, HR buyers as the first audience

## Example Dialogue

Domain expert: "Yes. Yoga supports the brand, but it is not the whole brand."

Developer: "Who are we speaking to first?"
